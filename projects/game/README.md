## Explanation of the setting.

It is the year 2100. Humanity has colonized the solar system. We are running out of space on 

Earth. Mars won’t be ready to accept human colonists at the scale that we need for another 

100 years. Venus will be ready twice that time. Humanity needs a better candidate for 

terraforming. There are none in the solar system. Humanity needs space and the only way to 

get it is to travel to other star systems. We sent out a probe to Alpha Centauri 50 years 

beforehand, when we first got our mass reduction drives that let us send machines at a quarter 

of light speed. Humans are far too squishy to use one of those. About 22 years later, we got our 

answer. A perfect world ready for us to colonize. The probe's atmospheric scanners confirmed 

it. Human ready oxygen levels, granitic levels, everything was there. There was primitive 

cyanobacteria there. Nothing that could harm us. We would have to genetically modify plants to 

grow there, but that was an easy task. There was only one problem: Humanity’s best ships 

could only travel at roughly 25% Lightspeed without killing people in special cryostasis 

chambers. Each could only bring a thousand at a time. It wasn’t enough. We needed FTL. We 

needed to try an idea Humanity had discarded in 2040- The voidshift drive, a drive that should, 

theoretically, send us into a dimension where the laws of distance were more forgiving, where 

physics could be, was, would be, bent into a pretzel. We need to go FTL: Into the Void.

## Intro text

A moment ago, the Prometheus, humanity’s first spaceship, was scheduled to make humanity’s 

first manned FTL flight with the Voidshift drive, taking us out of our dimension around 50 AU out 

from the sun and into the Void. Then it would use the fact that distance in the Void is flimsy at 

best, and not a factor more often than not to travel for a bit and come back out about a 100 AU 

from the sun. We had tried using probes before, but things always went wrong. Navigating the 

void made computers malfunction so badly that the routes they plotted took them longer to 

traverse than if they’d just traveled through real space. The 10% of probes that came back at 

least. We’re still not sure what happened to the other 90%. They were malfunctioning so badly 

that they should’ve just crashed, indeed did crash exactly pi milliseconds after the rift to the 

void closed. We tried more and more complex AI’s, getting better every time, but it wasn’t 

enough. Then, just as we were about to give up on the Voidshift drive, a miracle happened. 

One of the probes took a path that required 1% less distance in void space than it did in real 

space.

Two minutes ago, the Prometheus activated its drive. When we opened up the probe, we saw 

that its motherboard had been infested with dog vomit slime. Looking at the logs, the slime, 

which can surprisingly think in the most rudimentary manner, had plotted a better route through 

the void than humanity’s best AI could running on quantum processors. The experiment was 

repeated with a better set up, integrating the mold into computers so it could think faster. 0.5% 

faster than in real space. Compared to the AI piloted probes which were twice as slow in void 

space, this was a breakthrough. A repeatable breakthrough.

A minute ago, a rift to the void was opened, a thing of swirling not-green, a color that everyone 

agreed was sort of like green but most definitely not green. After all, it didn't show up on the 

cameras. We tried using everything, and found that the smarter a creature was, the better it 

could pilot a ship. More experience meant better pilots, and some creatures just had an 

enormously good performance, the mold among them. It wasn’t enough. 90% of the chimp 

piloted probes were coming back from the 50 AU trek in about 500 minutes. We were so very 

close to the 415 minute barrier, the one that would let humanity declare itself FTL capable. So, 

we set out to take the logical next step. We set out to do a manned test flight.

A moment ago, we entered the void as the rift closed behind us. I wasn’t the test pilot. I wasn’t 

the navigator. I wasn’t the captain. I was just the guy who handled the chimps, an ordinary, only 

mildly important man among a crew of three hundred. They were meant to most people, but 

they loved me. Especially the ones that had been in the Void the most. I just got them, and as 

such any time they wanted to use the best chimps, they asked me. Bet you’ve never seen a 

chimp go on a strike before. Proper toilets solved that issue. I was there simply because my 

chimps would be. Time was different in the Void after all. Sometimes the probes had been in 

there for months. It was part of why we had such a big crew, the other reason being that we 

had only the faintest clue what we were doing, and only some people seemed to be able to 

understand the paths through the Void, just like only some creatures could. Oh, everyone could 

understand the maths if they were explained, but some creatures seemed to have an 

enormously good performance, and that was what we needed for FTL to actually be faster than 

light instead of just much faster than outside the dimension.

A moment ago, a wave of alien whispers entered my mind for the first time. Simulations 

wouldn’t work if they were about the void for all but the most basic of training. They hadn’t 

worked for the chimps, the only way that training seemed to work was to have a chimp with 

prior experience sit with another chimp for its first voyage through the Void. We were prepared 

to be here for a while, the only time the chimps had gotten that good a performance was after 

multiple test flights, and we needed to do better. We also brought a couple crack chimp pilots 

along in case nobody aboard could do it. That way, we could navigate out of the Void if all else 

failed. Some higher up had it instead on it. Later, that higher up would get a commendation for 

their foresight.

A moment ago, I was surrounded by tentacle monsters. In retrospect, how tense the chimps 

were, the way so many of them seemed to suddenly fall ill(I think they were faking it) and 

seemed to want me should’ve tipped me off. Only the ones that were the bravest seemed to be 

in any condition to come along. The moment that the rift shut, the moment everyone else 

turned into those things, things that made my head hurt as it gazed upon the impossible, 

seems like so long ago.

A moment ago, a tragedy happened. I am the only survivor. I need to get out of here if I want to 

tell their story. I can’t save them. I can save myself, and my poor chimps. I also seem to be able 

to think faster than I should because everything is barely moving. My head hurts, I can’t keep 

this up for much longer. All that is left is to reach the pilot helm and escape.

I know that I can do it.

There's just one promblem: I can't remember where anything is.

Whatever the heck happened scrambled my brain. Oh, and the gaint tentacle

monsters. Those are also a promblem.
